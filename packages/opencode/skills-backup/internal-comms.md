Write professional internal communications following company guidelines and preferred formats.

## Communication Types

**Use for:**
- 3P updates (Progress, Plans, Problems)
- Company newsletters
- FAQ responses
- Status reports
- Leadership updates
- Project updates
- Incident reports
- Team communications
- Policy announcements

## Guidelines by Type

### 3P Updates (Progress, Plans, Problems)
Format for team updates on project status:

**Structure:**
1. **Progress**: What was completed since last update
2. **Plans**: What will be done next
3. **Problems**: Any blockers or concerns

**Tone:** Professional, concise, action-oriented
**Length:** Brief, bullet points preferred
**Audience:** Team members, project stakeholders

### Company Newsletter
Format for company-wide communications:

**Structure:**
1. **Header**: Clear, engaging title
2. **Executive Summary**: Key highlights
3. **Main Content**: Organized sections
4. **Upcoming Events**: Forward-looking items
5. **Closing**: Call to action or contact info

**Tone:** Professional but accessible, inspiring
**Length:** Comprehensive but scannable
**Audience:** All employees

### FAQ Responses
Format for answering frequently asked questions:

**Structure:**
1. **Question Restatement**: Echo the ask
2. **Direct Answer**: Clear, concise response
3. **Additional Context**: Relevant details
4. **Follow-up Resources**: Where to learn more

**Tone:** Helpful, authoritative, patient
**Length:** Complete but not overwhelming
**Audience:** Individual asker, broader audience

### Status Reports
Format for project or team status updates:

**Structure:**
1. **Executive Summary**: Overall status (On Track/At Risk/Off Track)
2. **Key Metrics**: Quantifiable progress
3. **Recent Achievements**: Completed items
4. **Upcoming Milestones**: Next steps
5. **Risks/Issues**: Concerns and mitigation

**Tone:** Objective, data-driven, transparent
**Length:** Detailed enough for decision-making
**Audience:** Management, stakeholders

### Leadership Updates
Format for executive communication:

**Structure:**
1. **Context**: Situation background
2. **Decision**: What was decided and why
3. **Implications**: Impact on organization
4. **Next Steps**: Required actions
5. **Timeline**: When changes take effect

**Tone:** Authoritative, strategic, clear
**Length:** Comprehensive for decision context
**Audience:** Leadership team, managers

## General Guidelines

### Writing Process
1. **Identify type**: Match request to appropriate format
2. **Gather information**: Collect facts, metrics, quotes
3. **Structure content**: Follow type-specific guidelines
4. **Review tone**: Ensure appropriate voice
5. **Verify accuracy**: Check facts and data
6. **Edit for clarity**: Remove jargon, simplify

### Tone Guidelines
- **Professional but approachable**
- **Clear and direct**
- **Respectful of time**
- **Action-oriented when needed**
- **Transparent about challenges**

### Formatting
- Use clear headings and subheadings
- Bullet points for lists
- Short paragraphs (3-4 sentences max)
- White space for readability
- Consistent style throughout
